About Kazakhstan

By Arad D.

General Overview:

Kazakhstan is the world's largest landlocked country, located in Central Asia and spanning both Europe and Asia. It is known for its vast steppes, rich natural resources, and diverse cultural heritage. Kazakhstan was part of the Soviet Union until gaining independence in 1991, and its capital is Astana (formerly known as Nur-Sultan). The country operates under a presidential republic system and has made significant economic progress due to its oil, gas, and mineral industries. Culturally, Kazakhstan is a blend of nomadic traditions and modern influences, with deep roots in Turkic and Mongol history.

Fun Fact!

It is home to the world’s first and largest space launch facility, the Baikonur Cosmodrome! Built by the Soviet Union in the 1950s, it was from this very site that Yuri Gagarin was launched into space in 1961, becoming the first human to journey into outer space. Baikonur remains one of the most important spaceports today, used by both Russia and international space agencies.

Borat Controversy:

Kazakhstan gained significant, albeit controversial, global attention with Borat films. Despite all the criticism towards the movie, they also increased interest in the country, with Kazakhstan’s tourism board later embracing the "Very Nice!" catchphrase from the film to promote tourism.

Kazakhstan's top attractions include the stunning Charyn Canyon and the Altai Mountains for nature lovers, and the scenic Caspian Sea coast for beach escapes. Almaty offers vibrant markets and the famous Medeu ice skating rink, while historical Silk Road sites like Turkestan feature the UNESCO-listed Mausoleum of Khoja Ahmed Yasawi.
